DELECTABLE MALAYSIAN CUISINES

Malaysian cuisine is known for its rich and diverse flavors. As a multicultural society, Malaysia has inherited a wide range of cuisines and even created some of its own fusion blends that are unique and characterizing for Malaysian foods. The vast majority of Malaysia's population can roughly be divided among three major ethnic groups which is Malays, Chinese and Indians. The remainder consists of the indigenous peoples of Sabah and Sarawak in East Malaysia, the Orang Asli of Peninsular Malaysia, the Peranakan and Eurasian creole communities, as well as a significant number of foreign workers and expatriates.

MALAYSIAN MALAY CUISINE

NASI LEMAK

  • Nasi lemak is a one of the popular food in Malaysia.
  • The special of this food is the main highlight is the rich and fragrant rice that is cook with coconut milk before steaming.
  • Then it is served with a variety of local dishes which is sambal chili, boiled eggs,salted peanut and some cucumber slices. This Nasi lemak is a very popular amongst different cultures and is commonly found in Southest Asian countries, the countries are Brunei,Indonesia and Singapore.
  • The Nasi Lemak is can be found with variety of dishes such as spicy chicken, fried chicken, chili prawn, chili squid and more.

    • SATAY

  • Satay is the authenthic food which is served in skewered meat.
  • It is were grilled over a charcoal flame to capture a smoked effect on it.
  • Satay is can comes in a many choice of dish which are beef, chicken or lamb.
  • It is also served with the peanut sauce that is very delicious.
  • The satay is available in most place in Malaysia. If you spot a stall that smoking with glorious barbecue that is confirm the satay stall.

    MURTABAK

  • Murtabak is a local version of stuffed omelet pancakes.
  • This Murtabak was originally an Indian Muslim cuisine,it is stuffed with minced meat,onions and spicy sauces before being pan-fried.
  • Murtabak is eaten as a snack, it because is it essentially a street food that it easy to eat on the go.

    • MALAYSIAN CHINESE CUISINE

    CHEE CHEONG FUN

  • Chee cheong fun is a square rice sheets made from a viscous mixture of rice flour and water.
  • It is usually servwd with tofu stuffed with fish paste.
  • The dish is eaten with accompaniment of semi sweet fermented bean paste sauce, chili paste or light vegetable curry gravy.
  • In Ipoh and Penang have different variants of the dish that is in Ipoh serve the dish with a red sweet sauce, thinly sliced pickled green chilies and fried shallots, while in Penang serve wit a type of sweet, black shrimp sauce that called hae ko.

    • HOKKIEN MEE

  • Hokkien mee actually has two variants with each being ubiquitous to a particular region of Peninsular Malaysia.
  • Penang Hokkien Mee reffered to in Penang as Hokkien Mee, is also known as hae mee elsewhere in Malaysia.
  • One of Penang's most famous specialties, it is a noodle soup with yellow and rice noodles immersed in a aromatic stock made from prawns and chicken for halal versions and it will garnished with a bolied egg, poached prawns, chopped kangkung and a dollop of spicy sambal

    MALAYSIAN INDIAN CUISINE

    CAPATHI

  • Capathi is a North Indian style flatbred.
  • It is made from dough of atte flour, water and salt by rolling the dough out into discs of approximatetly twelve centimetres in diameter and browning the discs on both sides on a very hot, dry tava or frying pan without any oil.
  • This capathi is usually eaten with curry.

    • ROTI TISSUE

  • Roti tissue in variant made as thin as a piece of 40-50 cm round shaped tissue in density.Then it is carefully folded by the cook into a tall, conical shape and left to stand upright. This Roti tissue served with curry gravy, chutneys and dal. Then finished off with sweet substance such as caramelised sugar and it is eaten as a dessert.
  • Roti tissue is available at most local Mamak stalls in Malaysia
